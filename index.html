A Practical First Step

It Does Not Need to Be Complicated.

Every business is different, but the first opportunity is often easier to find than people expect. It usually starts with one recurring bottleneck, one manual process, or one decision point that is consuming more time than it should.

Scenario 01

Compliance Review Preparation

A mid-sized accounting firm has senior staff spending hours preparing for compliance reviews. The first step could be to map the current workflow, identify where time is being lost, and design a practical AI-supported process to prepare the information faster and more consistently.

The goal is not to replace expertise. It is to give experienced people more time to apply it.

Scenario 02

Client Onboarding

A financial advisory practice is dealing with too much back-and-forth during client onboarding. The first step could be to identify the repetitive admin tasks, standardise the information flow, and automate the parts of the process that do not require professional judgement.

A smoother experience for clients and less manual follow-up for the team.

Scenario 03

Knowing Where to Start With AI

A tax and business advisory firm knows AI matters but does not have a clear plan. The first step could be a structured discovery session to identify the highest-value opportunities, assess what is practical, and prioritise one or two areas that can be tested without major disruption.

The aim is not a large AI strategy document. It is to find a sensible starting point.

The common thread: none of these starts with a major transformation program. They start with a focused conversation about where time, cost, or capacity is being lost — and what could be improved first.
